Divine Comedy

 

The Divine Comedy is an epic poem written by the Italian poet Dante Alighieri, completed in 1320. The epic poem is divided into 3 parts: Inferno, Purgatory and Paradiso. The Divine Comedy takes the reader into a journey in which Dante has to go through Hell, and the Purgatory in order to get to paradise, which is the place where his beloved Beatrice resides. Dante is not alone in this journey, he will be led by his admire Roman poet, Virgil. Virgil was comissioned by Beatriz to hel Dante return to her presence. While in his journey, Dante describes with great detail everything that he sees. Throughout his journey he often feels discourage to continue his journey, but if is always push to continue by Virgil and the reminder that he will return with Breatiz.

Inferno

 

Inferno, which translates to hell in English, is by far the most popular section of the Divine Comedy. In fact, this section of the book was so influencial among the christian faith, that a lot of the images, and ideas that we have today about hell come from this section. Inferno is divided into 9 circles: Limbo, Lust, Gluttony, Greed, Wrath, Heresy, Violence, Fraud and Treachery. The deeper the circle in hell, the greater the sin and therefore the greater the punishment. This part of the book was also the most controvesial of all the parts of the book. So much so, that this will forced Dante into exile. Why? Because he questioned a lot of the beliefs of the catholic church at the time. Also, Dante put a lot of the popes of his times in the deeper circles of hell.

Purgatory

 

Purgatorio, is the second step of Dante's trip to Purgatorio (purgatory). This part of the book, is the most lyrical of all the parts. Purgatorio is divided into 7 circles: Proud, Envious, Wrathful, Slothful, Covetous, Gluttonous, and Lustful. This 7 circles correspond to the 7 deadly sins of the catholic theology. In this part, Dante witnesses how souls atone their sins. The difference between Purgatory and Inferno is that in Purgatory, souls have hope that they one day will go to heaven to live with God, in Inferno, as the entrance remarked, there is no hope.

Paradiso

 

Paradiso is the final stop for Dante. In paradiso Dante finally reunites with his beloved Beatriz. This part of the Divine Comedy is consider the most theological. Here Dante witnesses many well known Catholic characters like St.Peter, St.Bernardo and even Jesus Christ. Paradiso is also divided into different circles: Moon, Mercury, Venus, the Sun, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, the Fixed Stars, and the Primum Mobile. The higher the cicle, the greter the glory.
